FINAL DEMO SCRIPT — CHOOSEWISE (4–6 MINUTES)

1. VALUE PROPOSITION OPENING (45–60 seconds)
ChooseWise is for students and other everyday users who struggle with decisions that involve multiple tradeoffs, like choosing an apartment, a class schedule, a job option, or a major purchase. The problem is that people often make these decisions in a messy way, where they are comparing things in their head and second-guessing themselves. ChooseWise solves that by combining weighted scoring with an AI-generated recommendation, so the user gets both structure and a plain-language explanation. That matters because it makes decisions faster, clearer, and easier to trust.

2. LIVE PRODUCT WALKTHROUGH (2–2.5 minutes)
Here I am entering two options. For this example, I’ll compare two apartments. Then I add criteria like cost, commute, safety, and space. Next, I assign weights based on how important each factor is, and I score both options from 1 to 10. When I click Analyze, the app calculates the weighted totals and then generates an AI recommendation. The success case here is that the user can quickly see both the numeric result and the reasoning behind it, instead of just getting a raw score with no explanation.

3. CUMULATIVE PROGRESS (1.5–2 minutes)
The first major enhancement is weighted scoring. I added this because simple comparison was too vague, and this change made the output much more structured and useful. The second major enhancement is the AI recommendation layer using the OpenAI API. I made this change because users need more than just numbers; they also want a short explanation, possible risks, and what to think about next. The third enhancement is the improved UX and layout. I cleaned up the interface so the workflow is easier to understand during the demo, and the result is that the app feels much more usable and presentation-ready. The fourth enhancement is deployment and security readiness. I restructured the app so the API key stays on the backend and never appears in the frontend or repository, which makes it safe to publish and easier to deploy as a real live demo.

4. REFLECTION CLOSE (30–45 seconds)
What worked well is combining structured scoring with AI reasoning, because that gives the user both clarity and explanation. What did not work as well is that this MVP still only handles two options at a time, so it is limited in scope. If I kept developing this as a real launch, I would add support for more than two options, saved decision histories, and possibly collaborative sharing so multiple people could weigh in on the same decision.
